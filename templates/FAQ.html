Frequently Asked Questions

Fake news is a type of false information deliberately spread through traditional or social media platforms with the intention to deceive people. It can be in the form of a hoax, satire, propaganda, or misleading content.
There are several ways to detect fake news, such as:
  • Check the source and credibility of the website or article
  • Verify the information with other reputable sources
  • Look for any biases or agendas behind the news
  • Watch out for sensational or clickbait headlines
Our fake news detection tool is a machine learning model that analyzes news articles and provides a probability score indicating the likelihood of the news being fake or genuine. The model uses various features such as language, source, content, and context to make its prediction.

Our fake news detection tool has an accuracy of around 95%, which means it can correctly identify fake news with a high degree of confidence. However, it is not perfect, and there may be cases where it fails to identify fake news or labels genuine news as fake.
